What is Capitalist Realism?

"It is easier to imagine the end of the world than capitalism"

Capitalist Realism is like a pervasive atmosphere, conditioning not only the production of culture but also as a regulation of work and education, acting as a kind of invisible barrier constraining thought and action.

From Mark Fisher's analysis (noted April 9): CR can only be threatened if it is shown to be inconsistent or untenable.

Key Characteristics

  • Paralysis of imagination: Inability to conceive of alternatives to capitalism
  • Reality principle: Ideological constructs presented as "empirical" facts
  • Atmosphere/Vibe: Present in all of life, creating paralysis to action and hope
  • Inevitability: Poverty, war, famine, and injustice presented as inevitable

Mark Fisher's Framework

Capitalist Realism emerged in the 1980s during the Reagan-Thatcher era. This was when Capitalist Realism was "fought for and established."

"Capitalism has colonized the dreaming life of the population"

Historical Context

The "End of History" narrative following the fall of the Soviet Union was accepted subconsciously. There isn't a need for anything else, we already have it. This left America "infantile and obese ‚Äî tacky, violent, scared."

Capitalism caught stride around the same time as the demystification of the world ‚Äî God is dead! What is left is being-as-consumer-spectator.

Business Ontology

Everything exists as a business does. This creates a fundamental shift in how we understand reality itself.

Three Areas of Attack

CR can only be threatened if shown to be inconsistent or untenable in these domains:

1. Environmental Catastrophe

Capitalism is going to exterminate us. The climate crisis reveals the fundamental unsustainability of infinite growth on a finite planet.

2. Mental Health

The psychosocial costs of capitalism manifest in epidemic levels of depression, anxiety, and suicide. "Depression costs the world 1 trillion in productivity" ‚Äî what a sad quote.

3. Bureaucracy

Late Stage Capitalism repeats Stalinism in valuing symbols of achievement over actual achievement. "Triumphs of publicity!"

The Problem: Privatizing issues brought by Late Stage Capitalism neuters collective response. Individual "solutions" to systemic problems.

Wall-E and Inter-passivity

"Wall-E performs our anti-capitalism for us, allowing us to continue to consume with impunity"

Robert Pfaller's concept of Inter-passivity: By watching (and enjoying‚Äîit is a good film) such an obvious example of the negative/bad/future consequence of capitalism, we participate and interact with the subjugation.

The film performs our anticapitalism for us. This releases guilt and suppresses what we have seen and know, allowing us to, in clear conscience, continue infinite consumption.

The most gothic, wretched depiction of Capital is its most accurate representation. Capital is both:

  • Hyper abstract and impersonal structure
  • Nothing without our cooperation

Related Concepts & Connections

Lacan's Real

For Lacan, the Real (desire) is what any "reality" suppresses. Reality constitutes itself through this repression.

The Real is an unrepresentable "X," a traumatic void that can only be glimpsed in the fractures and inconsistencies in the field of "apparent reality."

Strategy: Invoking the Real to show the reality Capitalism presents through:

  1. Environmental Catastrophe
  2. Mental Health
  3. Bureaucracy

Experiencing the Real through threshold crossings. Violations. Must be visceral, not condescending.
